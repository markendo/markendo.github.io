About Me

Mark Endo

I am a senior at Stanford University studying computer science in the artificial intelligence track. Broadly, my research focuses on methods that learn useful and generalizable representations without the need for extensive data collection. My published work is on using pre-trained language-image models for radiology report generation, developing data-efficient methods for chest X-ray segmentation, and exploring self-supervised pre-training of human motion forecasting for few-shot gait impairment severity estimation. Most recently, I am working on creating methods that have a fine-grain, complex understanding of motion via human trajectory question-answering.
